Moment 1: Problem Selection and Solution Brainstorming

Project Planning and Estimation Problems in Software Development

Context: Workplace Context

Problem Description

Project planning and estimation are essential activities in software development. However, many organizations face difficulties when calculating the time, resources, and effort required to complete a project successfully.

Teams often create unrealistic schedules or fail to consider possible risks during development. As a result, projects may suffer delays, budget overruns, unmet requirements, and lower software quality.

This issue affects both small and large technology companies and is considered one of the most common challenges in project management.

Who Is Affected?

  • Software developers
  • Project managers
  • Technology companies
  • Clients
  • End users

Why Is This Problem Important?

Good planning helps teams organize tasks, allocate resources, and establish realistic deadlines.

When estimations are inaccurate:

  • Project deadlines are missed.
  • Costs increase.
  • Team stress grows.
  • Software quality decreases.
  • Client satisfaction is affected.

Proposed Solutions

1. Use Story Points for Task Estimation

Story points are used in agile methodologies to measure the complexity, effort, and risk of a task instead of estimating only in hours.

  • Improves estimation accuracy.
  • Reduces pressure caused by unrealistic deadlines.
  • Supports better planning for complex projects.

2. Apply the Three-Point Estimation Technique

This technique considers three scenarios to calculate expected effort using a weighted average formula: E = (O + 4M + P) / 6.

  • Optimistic estimate (O)
  • Most likely estimate (M)
  • Pessimistic estimate (P)

Combining these values provides a more realistic and reliable estimate, reducing project risks and improving uncertainty management.

3. Use Historical Project Data

Analyzing previous projects helps teams compare estimated and actual completion times, improving future planning processes.

  • Supports data-driven decisions.
  • Reduces estimation mistakes.
  • Provides a better understanding of team capacity.

4. Implement Agile Methodologies and Project Management Tools

Methodologies such as Scrum and Kanban help teams organize work into smaller, manageable stages. Tools like Jira, Trello, and Microsoft Project improve task tracking and team collaboration.

  • Better project control.
  • Improved team communication.
  • Early identification of project issues.
