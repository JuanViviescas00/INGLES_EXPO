The Problem

Description

Project planning and estimation problems occur when teams incorrectly predict the time, effort, and resources needed to complete software projects.

These mistakes often lead to missed deadlines, increased costs, reduced software quality, and stress among team members.

Main Consequences

  • ⚠️ Missed deadlines & delay penalties
  • 💰 Budget overruns
  • 📉 Lower software quality
  • 🔥 Team burnout & high turnover
  • 💔 Unsatisfied clients

Possible Solutions

Story Points

Measure task complexity instead of estimating only by hours to reduce psychological pressure.

Three-Point Estimation

Use optimistic, pessimistic, and most likely scenarios to find a realistic statistical balance.

Historical Data

Analyze metrics from previous sprints and completed projects to improve future baseline estimations.

Agile Methodologies

Apply Agile, Scrum, and Kanban frameworks to manage progressive scope adjustments efficiently.

Project Logbook

Logbook Entry #1 — Problem Exploration (Moment 1)

1. What problem did I choose and why?

I chose the workplace problem: Problems in project planning or estimation. Incorrectly predicting time, effort, and resources is extremely common. I selected it because estimation errors directly lead to team stress, missed deadlines, and compromise client trust.

2. What are the possible solutions I identified?

I identified four primary solutions: Story Points (Agile Estimation) based on task complexity; Three-Point Estimation using optimistic, pessimistic, and likely scenarios; Historical Data Analytics comparing velocity metrics; and Agile Methodologies for progressive scope adjustments.

3. What questions or doubts do I have?

How can a junior developer negotiate a realistic estimate when managers push for aggressive deadlines? How do we balance historical data with brand new technologies that the team has never used before?

Logbook Entry #2 — Design & Prototype (Moment 2)

1. What HTML structure did I design and why?

I designed a semantic single-page structure using <header>, <nav>, <main>, <section>, <article>, and <footer>. This ensures accessibility (screen reader support), search engine optimization (SEO), and a clean stylesheet structure.

2. What CSS strategies am I planning (Flexbox, Grid, colors, spacing)?

I am planning an HSL-based color palette (slate blue and electric indigo) for a modern feel, Flexbox for one-dimensional layouts (nav links alignment, header), CSS Grid for the solutions cards, and Glassmorphism for a premium semi-transparent navigation bar.

3. What challenges appeared?

The main challenges were making the cards collapse into a single column on mobile screens and styling input focus states. I solved these by implementing media queries (@media (max-width: 768px)) and custom focus transitions.

4. What feedback did I receive after socialization?

My classmates praised the typography and color scheme. They suggested adding interactive hover animations to the cards and button to make the UI feel more active and responsive, which has been successfully added to our CSS stylesheet.
